# AWS expanded clock benchmarks

Date: 2026-05-07

All successful values are `ns/op`. Every successful row had `confidence_high=true` in the raw JSON. `panic` means the guarded candidate rejected the environment. `SIGSEGV`/`SIGILL` means a fault-isolated direct counter is not executable in that environment. `n/a` means the candidate is not compiled for that target.

## Key readouts

- `CLOCK_MONOTONIC_RAW` did not produce a meaningful speed win over `CLOCK_MONOTONIC` on these AWS runs.
- Direct vDSO is a small win on some targets, especially i686, but libc `clock_gettime` is already close to vDSO on x86_64 and aarch64.
- Forced `clock_gettime` syscalls are much slower than libc/vDSO paths and should stay fallback-only.
- `RDTSCP` and `LFENCE; RDTSC` are consistently slower than plain `RDTSC`; they buy ordering, not speed.
- PMU/cycle counters (`RDPMC`, `PMCCNTR_EL0`) are not reliable `Instant` candidates. They either failed here or represent CPU-work counters rather than elapsed time.
